About Me

I am a first-year PhD student in computer science at the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 2019 fall semester. I am now doing research at madPL group, supervised by Loris D'Antoni and Aws Albarghouthi.

I received my B.S. in Computer Science at Peking University (PKU), supervised by Yingfei Xiong. I visited Professor Tao Xie's group at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign past summer. Also, I worked as a research intern in Software Analytics Group at Microsoft Research Asia mentored by Shi Han.

I am good at algorithms and data structures. I was one of the teaching assistants of Algorithm Design and Analysis in 2018 spring semester. I was a contestant of ACM-ICPC and I had won four gold medals at Regional and China-Final contests. When I was in high school, I won a gold medal at the National Olympiad of Informatics.

I love swimming. My best record of 2km-swimming is 42 minutes. I find swimming is a good way to ease my anxiety. Currently, I focus on keeping fit and I go to gym three times a week. I also love cooking. I hope that I could open a blog to record my dishes throughout my PhD study. Yo estudio español.

Research Interest

My main research interest is software engineering and programming language, especially software analytics, repair, and programming synthesis. I am also interested in natural language processing. Recently I am focusing on testing, verifying, and understanding ML/DL approaches for software engineering tasks (such as program synthesis, document generation).
