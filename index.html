About Me

I am a senior student majoring in computer science at Peking University (PKU), supervised by Professor Yingfei Xiong. I will be joining the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 2019 fall semester.

I visited Professor Tao Xie's group at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign past summer. Also, I worked as a research intern in Software Analytics Group at Microsoft Research Asia mentored by Shi Han.

I am good at algorithms and data structures. I was one of the teaching assistants of Algorithm Design and Analysis in 2018 spring semester. I was a contestant of ACM-ICPC and I had won four gold medals at Regional and China-Final contests. When I was in high school, I won a gold medal at the National Olympiad of Informatics.

I love swimming. My best record of 2km-swimming is 42 minutes. I find swimming is a good way to ease my anxiety. I am now focus on keeping fit and I keep going to gym three times a week. I also love cooking. I hope that I could open a blog to record my dishes throughout my PhD study.

Research Interest

My main research interest is software engineering and programming language, especially software analytics, repair, and programming synthesis. Recently I am focusing on detecting numerical bugs in TensorFlow programs. I am also interested in natural language processing.
